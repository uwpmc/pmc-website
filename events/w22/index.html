Homomorphism-Homogeneous Graphs

Pure Math Talk by Professor Nickolas Rollick!

July 19, 2021 @ 5:00 PM–6:00 PM in MC 2066

Dearest mathematics enjoyers,

The Pure Math Club is proud to announce The First In-Person Event Held By Any Club And/Or Organization Ever: we're hosting a Pure Math Prof Talk with the Professor William Slofstra. The title is "Negami's Planar Cover Conjecture and Quantum Games". Here's an abstract:

Abstract. A graph (vertices and edges, not functions) is planar if it can be drawn on a sheet of paper without the edges crossing each other. Not every graph is planar; \(K_{3,3}\) is a famous counterexample. Some nonplanar graphs, including \(K_{3,3}\), can be drawn in the plane without edges crossing by allowing multiple copies of each vertex. A representation of this form is called a planar cover. Surprisingly, every graph we know with a planar cover has a planar double cover (which means it uses exactly two copies of every vertex). Negami's planar cover conjecture, which is now 30 years old, is that this is always the case: if a graph has a planar cover, then it has a planar double cover. In this talk, I'll explain this conjecture, and then explain another open problem in group theory / quantum information inspired by this conjecture.

The talk will be in MC 2066 on Thursday, November 18th at 4:00pm. Very little background required. Come on by and feel smart for an hour!
